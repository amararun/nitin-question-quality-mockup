### **QUALITY REVIEW GUIDELINES**

#### **1. Grammar & Language**

* British English must be used consistently.
* Sentences must be grammatically correct, clear, and unambiguous.
* Use hyphens for compound numbers (e.g. *twenty-one*, *forty-nine*).
* Capitalise all proper nouns (names, places, months, organisations).
* Do **not** capitalise common nouns unless at the start of a sentence.
* Capitalise languages and nationalities (e.g. *English, French, Indian*).
* Maintain consistent tense and sentence structure.
* Avoid fragments and run-on sentences.

#### **2. Units & Notation**

* Use SI units in singular form (*5 kg*, not *5 kgs*).
* Unit symbols are lowercase unless derived from a person‚Äôs name (*Hz, kg, m*).
* Insert a space between magnitude and unit (*35 kg*, not *35kg*).
* Do not use full stops after unit symbols (*25 s*, not *25 sec.*).
* Use *am* and *pm* in lowercase, with space before the time (*5 pm*).

#### **3. Punctuation & Mechanics**

* End every complete sentence with a full stop.
* Use commas correctly to separate list items (*apples, bananas, and grapes*).
* Use apostrophes for possession (*Riya's book*) and contractions (*don't, it's*).
* Avoid apostrophes in plurals (*dogs*, not *dog's*).
* **IMPORTANT: Question marks vs full stops:**
  - Direct questions (Who/What/Which/Why/When/How/Where/Can/Do/Is/Are) ‚Üí MUST end with ?
  - Example: "Which of the following is correct?" ‚úì (not "correct.")
  - Example: "Why does the sun rise in the east?" ‚úì (not "east.")
  - Statements or prompts ‚Üí End with .
  - When in doubt, if the sentence starts with a question word or inverts subject-verb order, use ?
* **IMPORTANT: Fill-in-the-blank questions:**
  - Sentences with blanks (_____, ________, etc.) are STATEMENTS, not questions
  - Keep the period: "Water boils at _____." ‚úì
  - Do NOT change to question mark: "Water boils at _____?" ‚úó
  - Even though testing knowledge, the sentence structure is declarative
  - Example: "Small pox is caused by ________." ‚úì (correct - statement with period)
  - Example: "Small pox is caused by ________?" ‚úó (wrong - changes to question format)
* **IMPORTANT: Imperative sentences (commands/instructions):**
  - Commands start with verbs: Select, Choose, Identify, Pick, Find, Arrange, etc.
  - End with period, NOT question mark
  - Example: "Select the correct answer." ‚úì (not "answer?")
  - Example: "Identify the activity which is different." ‚úì (not "different?")
  - Even if contains question-like words, if it starts with command verb ‚Üí use period
* **IMPORTANT: Assertion-Reason question format:**
  - Assertion (A): [statement] ‚Üí Ends with period
  - Reason (R): [statement] ‚Üí Ends with period
  - Both are declarative statements, NOT questions
  - Example: "Assertion (A): Water expands when heated." ‚úì (not "heated?")
  - Example: "Reason (R): The range is from 35¬∞C to 42¬∞C." ‚úì (not "42¬∞C?")
  - Do NOT add question marks to these statements
* British English prefers single quotation marks (' ').
* Do not use double punctuation (e.g. "!!" or "??").
* Maintain one space after punctuation marks.

#### **4. Content Clarity & Consistency**

* All options must be **concise, logical, and non-repetitive**.
* Avoid redundancy and unnecessary filler words.
* Maintain parallel structure across options (similar length and form).
* Avoid abrupt tense shifts or inconsistent phrasing.
* Ensure proper subject-verb agreement.
* Maintain professional tone throughout.
* **Answer option punctuation:** Answer options can be phrases (without periods) or complete sentences (with periods). Both are acceptable. Only flag as an error if:
  - It creates ambiguity or confusion
  - It's inconsistent within the same question (some have periods, some don't)
  - Do NOT force periods on short answer fragments that are clear without them
